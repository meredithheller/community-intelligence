# Community Voice Intelligence

## The Problem

Marketing teams know their brand voice and where their customers live online, but they often lack the community fluency to show up in those spaces without sounding corporate. The teams that win do so by joining the conversation natively, not forcing their well-defined brand voice in. But without in-house community experts, most teams have no repeatable system to do this at scale.

## The Hypothesis

If marketing teams could deeply understand the tone, language, and conversational norms of any community, they could engage authentically across many communities at once — driving customer acquisition without paid ads or templated outreach.

## The Solution

Paste your brand URL and a Hacker News thread. In seconds, the tool returns:

1. **A Community Intelligence Summary** — who's in this community, their tone, vocabulary, what formats land, and what gets ignored
2. **3 Channel-Native Response Options** — real comments you could post, voiced through your brand's expertise, written to fit the community natively

---

## How to Use It

### What you need
- Your brand's website URL
- A Hacker News thread URL

### Step 1 — Paste your brand URL

This is your company's homepage or product page. The tool reads it to understand what you do, who you serve, and how you talk.

Example: `https://airops.com`

### Step 2 — Paste a Hacker News thread URL

Find a thread where your potential customers are already having a relevant conversation. Copy the URL from your browser.

Example: `https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47161759`

*(This thread — "The happiest I've ever been" — is a good test case.)*

### Step 3 — Click Analyze Community

The tool runs a three-stage pipeline:
1. Reads your brand and the thread
2. Maps the community's tone, language, and what resonates
3. Writes 3 comments that fit the community and reflect your brand's expertise

### Step 4 — Review your response options

Each response includes:
- The comment text (ready to post)
- The psychological hook it uses
- Why that approach works for this specific community

You can copy individual responses or share the full set with a teammate via the share link.
